About Me

I am a dedicated Electrical and Computer Engineering student at Cornell University, driven by a passion for embedded systems, hardware design, and cutting-edge technology solutions. My academic journey is complemented by hands-on experience in both industry and research settings, where I've developed expertise in microelectronics, digital logic design, and embedded systems programming.

My professional experience spans from field engineering at Charter Communications - Spectrum, where I work with power distribution systems and network infrastructure, to hardware development with Engineers Without Borders, where I contribute to meaningful projects that create real-world impact. As a Teaching Assistant for Digital Logic and Computer Organization, I guide students through complex concepts in FPGA architecture and hardware design.

I thrive on solving complex technical challenges, from architecting RISC-V processors to developing intelligent embedded systems. My goal is to advance research in hardware-software co-design and contribute to the development of energy-efficient computing architectures that will shape the future of technology.
